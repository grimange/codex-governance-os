# Evidence Surface Alignment

## Session Registry

- The reconstruction rules anchor reconstruction on canonical `session_id`.
- The registry remains the identity and indexed summary surface and is not
  treated as a substitute for event history.

## Session Ledger

- The reconstruction rules treat the ledger as the durable event-evidence
  surface for what was recorded as having happened.
- The doctrine explicitly prohibits inventing events absent from durable
  evidence.

## Session State Machine

- The reconstruction rules defer lifecycle meaning to the state-machine canon.
- No alternative lifecycle states or transition semantics are introduced.

## Lifecycle Observation

- The reconstruction rules use lifecycle observation only for admissible signal
  normalization and clarification.
- Observation remains interpretive and subordinate.

## Runtime Boundary

- The reconstruction rules allow runtime context only when admitted through the
  runtime-boundary canon and mapped back into canonical fields.
- Runtime context remains bounded supporting evidence rather than authoritative
  lifecycle truth.
