# Insufficiency And Conflict Rule Verification

The canon explicitly defines handling for:

- insufficient evidence
- conflicting evidence
- missing evidence

It also explicitly includes the required sentence:

> Silence is not proof.

Restriction observed during verification:

- the lane definition names `ambiguous` and `stale` evidence explicitly
- ambiguity is handled implicitly through the conflict and unresolved-claim rule
- stale evidence is handled implicitly by the `non-evidence` category, where
  stale or superseded summaries are excluded from proof
- neither ambiguity nor stale evidence has its own dedicated interpretation
  subsection yet
